The Efficient Market Hypothesis is a Philosophical Razor

I’ve read some inane discussions about the validity of the efficient market hypothesis (EMH). I’ve even participated in some. Proponents warn that cocky retail traders are naive, and detractors point to historical examples of inefficiencies. I claim this is a category mistake by both parties. The EMH is more like a philosophical razor: you should accept it for a given asset, unless you have some information to the contrary.
